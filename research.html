Research

Methods for reliable, uncertainty-aware engineering intelligence

My research centers on statistical and machine-learning methods that support dependable inference, prediction, and decision-making in engineering systems.

Bayesian modeling

I am interested in probabilistic models that quantify uncertainty in a way that is useful for engineering decisions, especially in noisy and data-limited settings.

Survival analysis and prognostics

I study methods for time-to-event modeling, failure prediction, and degradation-informed analysis in reliability engineering contexts.

Physics-informed learning

I am drawn to models that combine scientific structure with flexible machine learning, especially when robustness and interpretability matter.
